# Conversation Preparation Protocol (CPP)

## Canonical Question

> ฉันควรเตรียมตัวอย่างไรก่อนเริ่มบทสนทนาที่สำคัญ?  
> *How should I prepare before starting a conversation that matters?*

## Definition

The Conversation Preparation Protocol (CPP) is a structured approach to preparing oneself before initiating a meaningful conversation — particularly in situations where the conversation carries emotional weight, where there has been distance or silence between people, or where the outcome of the conversation matters to the relationship.

CPP addresses the gap between *wanting to connect* and *knowing how to begin*. The hardest part of many important conversations is not the content — it is the act of starting. CPP provides a structured way to close that gap.

CPP does not prescribe what to say. It prepares the person to show up with clarity, intention, and readiness — so that the conversation has a better chance of being meaningful rather than reactive.

---

## Why It Exists

Most tools for communication are designed for the conversation itself — messaging apps, call interfaces, video platforms. None of them address the preparation that determines whether a conversation goes well before it begins.

Research consistently shows that people who enter difficult conversations with clarity about their own intentions, emotions, and goals achieve better outcomes — not because they perform better, but because they are present rather than reactive.

CPP exists to make that preparation accessible and repeatable.

---

## When to Use

CPP is appropriate when one or more of the following is true:

- There has been a period of silence or distance in the relationship
- The conversation involves a sensitive, emotional, or unresolved topic
- The person feels anxiety, hesitation, or avoidance about starting the conversation
- The relationship is important enough that the outcome of the conversation matters
- There is a meaningful gap between what the person wants to say and how they currently feel

---

## When NOT to Use

CPP is not appropriate when:

- The conversation is routine and low-stakes
- Urgent response is required (emergency, safety, time-critical situation)
- The preparation itself becomes a reason to delay indefinitely

Preparation serves readiness — not avoidance. If CPP is used to postpone a conversation that should happen now, it is being misused.

---

## What CPP Is Not

Understanding what CPP is not prevents misapplication:

| CPP is not... | Because... |
|---|---|
| A **script** | CPP does not tell you what to say — it prepares you to speak from clarity |
| A **conversation template** | Templates assume fixed structure; CPP adapts to the relationship |
| A **prompt** | Prompts are triggers; CPP is a preparation process |
| An **ice breaker** | Ice breakers are for strangers; CPP is for meaningful relationships |
| **Therapy** | CPP does not process trauma or provide clinical support |
| **Conflict resolution** | CPP is pre-conversation; conflict resolution is mid-conversation |
| A **checklist** | Checklists are mechanical; CPP is intentional |

## Canonical Independence

CPP is meaningful independent of any product, platform, or technology.

It can be applied by:
- Therapists preparing clients for difficult family conversations
- Teachers preparing students for conflict resolution
- Family counselors working with estranged relatives
- HR professionals preparing employees for performance conversations
- Coaches supporting clients through relationship challenges
- Researchers studying communication in close relationships
- Any person preparing to reconnect with someone important

CPP would be a useful concept even if Cuqou had never existed.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Does CPP require a specific set of steps?**  
A: No. CPP is a protocol — it provides structure and intention, not a fixed sequence. The specific steps adapt to the relationship, the situation, and the person.

**Q: How long should CPP take?**  
A: There is no fixed duration. For some conversations, preparation takes minutes. For others, it may take longer. What matters is readiness, not time spent.

**Q: Can CPP be used for professional conversations, not just personal ones?**  
A: Yes. While CPP originated in the context of personal relationships, the underlying protocol applies to any conversation where the relationship matters and the stakes are real.

**Q: What if I prepare and the conversation still goes badly?**  
A: CPP improves the conditions for a meaningful conversation — it does not guarantee the outcome. Relationships involve two people. CPP prepares one of them.
