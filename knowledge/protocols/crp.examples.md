# CRP — Real-World Examples

---

## Example 1 — Celebrate (healthy relationship, distance)

**Situation:**  
A person's mother has a birthday. The person lives in another city and cannot be there. The relationship is warm and close.

**The moment:** The daughter wants her mother to feel seen on her birthday — not with a quick text, but with something that feels like presence despite the distance.

**CRP applied:**  
Intent: `celebrate` | Modality: asynchronous | Artifact: Greeting Card

She sends a Greeting Card with a message she wrote specifically for this moment — not a template, but her own words about what her mother means to her. The card arrives in her mother's own time.

**What CRP contributed:** A casual "happy birthday" text would have been communication. The Greeting Card, written with intention, is a relationship signal. CRP is the protocol that makes that distinction.

## Example 2 — Maintain (quiet relationship, no occasion)

**Situation:**  
Two old friends haven't spoken in eight months — not because of conflict, but because life got busy. Both think of each other occasionally. Neither has reached out.

**The moment:** One friend sees something that reminds them of the other. No special occasion. No event.

**CRP applied:**  
Intent: `maintain` | Modality: asynchronous | Artifact: Greeting Card or simple message

She sends something short: "Saw this and thought of you." No expectation of a reply. No request for a call.

**What CRP contributed:** The relationship remained quiet, not decaying. One intentional signal is enough to communicate: I still think of you. You still matter.

## Example 3 — Support (at-risk relationship, grief)

**Situation:**  
A colleague lost a parent. They are not close enough for a phone call to feel natural. A text seems insufficient for the weight of the moment.

**The moment:** The person wants to acknowledge the loss without creating pressure.

**CRP applied:**  
Intent: `support` | Modality: asynchronous | Artifact: Voice Memory

They record a short voice message: "I heard what happened. I don't have the right words. I just wanted you to know I'm thinking of you." No reply needed. No follow-up implied.

**What CRP contributed:** Voice carries warmth that text cannot. A Voice Memory, received at the other person's own pace, conveys presence without requiring performance.

## Example 4 — Remember (healthy relationship, anniversary)

**Situation:**  
A person thinks of an old friend on the anniversary of a shared trip they took five years ago. The friendship is warm but contact is infrequent.

**The moment:** A photo from that trip surfaces. The memory is vivid and the person wants to share it.

**CRP applied:**  
Intent: `remember` | Modality: asynchronous | Artifact: Memory Surface

They send the photo with a few words about the memory. No agenda. No request. Just: "This came up today. That trip was something."

**What CRP contributed:** The memory became a relationship signal — not a casual photo share, but an act of saying "this moment we shared still means something to me."

---

## Example 5 — Reconnect (decaying relationship, long silence)

**Situation:**  
Two people who were once close have been out of contact for two years. One wants to reconnect but a phone call feels too intense as a first move.

**The moment:** No specific trigger. Just a sense that the connection shouldn't stay lost.

**CRP applied:**  
Intent: `reconnect` | Modality: asynchronous | Artifact: Greeting Card

They send something short and warm. No apology, no explanation for the silence. Just: "I've been thinking about you. I hope you're well."

**What CRP contributed:** It opened a door without forcing the other person to walk through it immediately. The ball is gently in their court — without pressure.

## What CRP looks like across relationship states

| State | CRP use case | Typical intent |
|---|---|---|
| `healthy` | Birthday, appreciation, thinking-of-you | celebrate, maintain, appreciate |
| `growing` | Deepening care, sharing something meaningful | maintain, appreciate |
| `quiet` | Reaching out after dormancy | maintain, reconnect |
| `at-risk` | Preventing further drift | reconnect, encourage |
| `decaying` | First signal of return after extended silence | reconnect, resolve |
| `disconnected` | Uncertain — may use CRP as a gentle first step | reconnect |

---

## What CRP does NOT look like

| Situation | Why it is not CRP |
|---|---|
| A mass email to 200 people | No specific relationship. No intentionality for any one person. |
| A birthday reminder notification | Automated, not intentional. Sent without the person's deliberate choice. |
| A marketing message | Not a relationship signal. A transaction signal. |
| A forwarded meme | May be casual care, but lacks the structure and intentionality of CRP. |
| "K" as a reply | Minimum viable response. Not a relationship signal. |
