# CPP — Design History and Reasoning

This document records *why* the Conversation Preparation Protocol was defined the way it was. It is not for end users. It is for future maintainers, researchers, and AI systems that need to understand the decisions behind the canonical definition.

---

## Why the name "Protocol"

The word "Protocol" was chosen deliberately over several alternatives:

| Word considered | Why it was rejected |
|---|---|
| **Framework** | A framework is a structure for thinking — CPP is a structure for doing. Frameworks invite interpretation; protocols invite practice. |
| **Method** | "Method" implies a single correct path. CPP adapts to context — the word "method" would have created the false impression that there is one right way. |
| **Guide** | A guide is advisory. CPP is intentional and structured. Calling it a guide would reduce its weight. |
| **Template** | Templates imply fill-in-the-blank completion. CPP is not a form — it is a process of self-preparation. |
| **Ritual** | This was considered seriously. A ritual captures the intentionality of CPP. It was rejected because "ritual" carries cultural and spiritual connotations that would limit CPP's applicability in clinical and professional contexts. |
| **Practice** | "Practice" implies repetition over time. CPP is situational — used before a specific conversation, not as an ongoing habit. |

**"Protocol" was chosen because:**
- It implies structure without rigidity
- It is used across domains: medicine, diplomacy, engineering, communication
- It signals that CPP is designed for consistent application, not improvisation
- It carries the appropriate weight — a protocol is taken seriously

---

## Why "Conversation Preparation" and not "Communication Preparation"

"Communication" is broader than "Conversation." Communication includes written messages, signals, non-verbal cues, and long-form correspondence.

CPP was designed specifically for **the moment before a real-time conversation begins** — a phone call, a face-to-face meeting, a video call. The word "Conversation" anchors the protocol to that specific moment.

"Communication Preparation" would have expanded scope beyond what CPP was designed to address.

---

## The core problem CPP was designed to solve

During the development of Cuqou, a pattern emerged repeatedly:

> People who cared about a relationship were not starting conversations — not because they lacked love or intention, but because they lacked a starting point.

The emotional friction of beginning a difficult or important conversation was being treated as a personal failure, rather than as a solvable problem.

CPP was created to name and address this specific friction: **the gap between wanting to connect and knowing how to begin.**

This is not a new human problem. What is new is naming it precisely enough that it can be addressed systematically.

---

## Why "Protocol" rather than a proprietary name

Early drafts used names like "Cuqou Start Protocol" or "Connection Preparation System." These were rejected because they tied the concept to the product.

The decision was made to use neutral, descriptive language — "Conversation Preparation Protocol" — so that the concept could exist independently of Cuqou, be cited by researchers, and be understood without product knowledge.

This decision aligns with Principle 9 of the Cuqou Ontology Constitution (Canonical Independence).

---

## What was deliberately excluded from the definition

Several elements were considered for inclusion and rejected:

**Steps or stages:** Early versions of CPP included a numbered sequence (e.g., "Step 1: identify your intention. Step 2: identify your emotion."). These were removed from the canonical definition because they made CPP feel like a checklist rather than a protocol. Specific implementations (such as Cuqou's app) may include steps — but the canonical concept should not prescribe them.

**Digital context:** The definition was deliberately kept free of references to messaging apps, notifications, or digital tools. CPP should remain meaningful in an analog world.

**Relationship type:** Early drafts specified "personal relationships." This was removed because CPP applies to professional relationships as well. The word "meaningful" was chosen to capture the quality of the relationship without restricting the type.

---

## Relationship to CRP

CPP and CRP were designed as a pair — pre-conversation and mid-conversation. However, they were defined separately and given distinct identities because:

1. CPP can be used without CRP (not every prepared conversation reaches conflict)
2. CRP can be needed without prior CPP (conversations sometimes escalate unexpectedly)
3. Treating them as one protocol would have obscured their distinct functions

The relationship between CPP and CRP is noted in each concept's related concepts section, but they are canonical peers — not sub-components of each other.

---

## Open questions at time of canonicalization

The following questions were unresolved when CPP v1.0 was canonicalized. They are recorded here for future review:

1. Should CPP have a defined minimum — a single essential element that must always be present for preparation to count as CPP?
2. Is there a "CPP for groups" — preparation for multi-person conversations?
3. How does CPP relate to therapeutic concepts like "pre-session journaling" or "intention setting"? Should those be noted as related external concepts?
