# CPP — Real-World Examples

Examples are separated from the canonical definition intentionally. Definitions should remain stable. Examples may evolve as new contexts emerge.

---

## Example 1 — Adult child reconnecting with a parent

**Situation:**  
A 35-year-old has not spoken to their father in 8 months following a misunderstanding at a family gathering. They want to reconnect but do not know how to start.

**Without CPP:**  
They draft and delete the same message three times. The silence continues for another month.

**With CPP:**  
Before reaching out, they spend time clarifying:  
- What do I actually want from this conversation? (Connection, not apology)
- What am I afraid will happen? (Being rejected again)  
- What does my father likely feel? (Also hurt, probably also missing contact)
- What is the one thing I want him to know?

They send a short message. Not perfect. But sent.

**What CPP contributed:** Clarity of intention reduced the emotional barrier enough to take action.

## Example 2 — Partners after a period of emotional distance

**Situation:**  
Two partners have been physically present but emotionally distant for several weeks. Neither has brought it up. The silence has started to feel normal.

**Without CPP:**  
One partner brings it up impulsively during an unrelated argument. The conversation becomes defensive.

**With CPP:**  
The partner who wants to address the distance takes time first — not to plan a script, but to understand what they actually feel and what they hope the conversation will accomplish. They choose a moment when both are calm. They begin with what they noticed, not what they blame.

**What CPP contributed:** The preparation shifted the conversation from reactive to intentional.

## Example 3 — Professional context: HR preparing for a difficult conversation

**Situation:**  
An HR professional needs to have a performance conversation with a long-tenure employee whose work has declined.

**Without CPP:**  
The meeting happens because it is scheduled. The HR professional enters with the documentation but without clarity on what they want the employee to feel and understand by the end.

**With CPP:**  
The HR professional prepares by asking: What is the outcome I need? What does this person need to hear in order to receive it? What assumptions am I carrying that might not be accurate?

**What CPP contributed:** The conversation becomes purposeful rather than procedural.

## Example 4 — Reconnecting with an old friend

**Situation:**  
Two people who were close friends lost contact over 3 years due to geography and life changes. One wants to reach out but feels the gap is now too large to bridge casually.

**Without CPP:**  
They write a long message explaining everything that happened, why they lost touch, and apologizing. The message feels heavy. It is never sent.

**With CPP:**  
They simplify. What do I actually want to say? "I miss you and I've been thinking about our friendship." What am I afraid of? "That they've moved on." What is one small, honest thing I can send?

A short message is sent. The friendship resumes.

**What CPP contributed:** Preparation reduced the conversation to its essential human truth.

## Contexts where CPP applies

| Context | Example situation |
|---|---|
| Family | Reconnecting after conflict, silence, or distance |
| Partnership | Addressing emotional distance before it becomes permanent |
| Friendship | Reaching out after years of lost contact |
| Professional | Difficult feedback, sensitive performance conversations |
| Grief | Talking to someone who has experienced a loss |
| Apology | Preparing to take responsibility without defensiveness |
| Reconciliation | Returning to a relationship after a serious breach |

---

## Contexts where CPP does not apply

| Context | Reason CPP is not appropriate |
|---|---|
| Emergency | No time for preparation; act immediately |
| Casual daily conversation | No emotional weight; preparation is unnecessary overhead |
| Debate or negotiation | CPP is not designed for adversarial contexts |
| Therapy session | The therapist facilitates; CPP is self-directed preparation |
